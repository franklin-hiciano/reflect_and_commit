Why it matters

Two versions of tomorrow.

A typical night a month ago

Go to bed with plans to lock-in on homework tomorrow. I wake up a bit late, eat brunch and shower, then do some. After an hour, I'm unfocused and go outside with my mom, for two, then three (because why not). I get home and, tired, lie down and listen to music. In the evening I get some more work done, but lose focus again. I go watch some YouTube and go to bed having done little. Really frustrated, I hope to finish it tomorrow.

An actual night from my own tree — real

Knowing I have trouble focusing once I'm out and about, I step out of bed and sit down to work. To fund ads for this app, I'd sold my monitor on eBay — the payout was sitting in a multi-week hold, so instead of waiting I worked the buyer directly until they released it early. A coding session that should've ended at midnight ran to sunrise and turned into a script that automated part of my product sourcing. None of it happens without the reflection that made tomorrow feel urgent.

That same night, traced forward — also real, not a hypothetical

A deadline I'd been avoiding came into focus → so I worked a session that ran past sunrise → which became a script that automated part of my sourcing → which paid for the AI tools I used to build faster → which tripled my output → which is the reason this app exists as a working beta and not still an idea. One night of honest questions, traced all the way to the product you're looking at right now.

What it's actually for

Most decisions don't matter. A few change everything.

Every day hands you choices — what to focus on, who to dedicate your attention to, etc. Almost all of them distract you from explicitly working on one thing you care about, that would make all the difference. The tree's job is to help you see those decisions the night before, not years later.

How a night actually goes

Three steps. The hard one is the point.

01

Design your tree

Write the questions that extract the truth from you. Start from the one we hand you, then make it yours, one line at a time.

02

Reflect

Press reflect and answer one card at a time. The tree branches on your answers and shows you last week's patterns as you go.

03

The day after

One commitment, one date. The next day, a clean yes or no — no streaks to game, just honesty with yourself.

This isn't a two-minute streak app. A real reflection runs 10–40 minutes and asks you things you'd rather skip. That's the design — the discomfort is where tomorrow gets decided. You won't be staring at a blank page, but you will have to be honest.
