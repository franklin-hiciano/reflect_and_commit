Why it matters

Two versions of tomorrow.

My typical night a month ago

Go to bed with plans to lock-in on homework tomorrow. Wake up a bit late, eat lunch and shower. Do some homework, unfocused after an hour. Go outside with mom, stay an extra hour (because why not). Get home. Wait till later to get some work done, lose focus again. Watch TV, go to bed. Say I'll finish the work tomorrow.

An actual day after 2-3 weeks of self-observation

Make a plan for tomorrow, make hard decisions, go to sleep. Step out of bed and start work instantly, knowing I'd get distracted otherwise. Fix a situation with a customer for my ebay business. Get hungry but go run before eating (knowing I'd get too lethargic to run). Difficult conversation with my parents about skipping college (decided the night before). Keep coding this app and run some ads. Reflect again, go to bed.

That same day, traced forward

Those ads ban me from TikTok → struggle to find a new product → reflecting, realize I can also sell this app → pull an all nighter to polish my prototype → sales during my graduation rehearsal → launch this product page and video tutorials → show my extended family → they think I'm "legit" → they agree to support me and fund my dream of making it without college
